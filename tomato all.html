Pepper

A plant up to 60 cm high with branched stems, elliptical leaves, large white or grayish flowers with purple spots. The fruits are berries with a low-juicy pericarp from spherical to trunk-shaped, from yellow and red to black-olive.

Red pepper has a strong spicy aroma and taste ranging from spicy to spicy and even very hot (due to the content of the phenolic compound capsaicin (decylenic acid vanillylamide), which is not found in sweet bell peppers). Capsaicin is found in the seeds, veins, and skin of the fruit.

Red peppers contain a large amount of vitamins C and A. Green peppers (unripe red peppers) contain significantly less of these vitamins. In addition, peppers are a good source of B vitamins and vitamin B6 in particular. Red pepper is also rich in potassium, magnesium and iron. In addition to capsaicin, 1.5% essential oil, fatty oil, was found in the fruits.

Agirdera pepper breeding programs are focusing on three main segments: Greenhouse Indeterminate pepper, Open-field Indeterminate Pepper, and Bush Type Open-field Pepper.

Growing hot peppers

Red peppers can also be grown at home in flower pots. A necessary condition is regular watering with periodic top dressing with a flower mixture.

Benefits of hot pepper

Due to the large amount of capsaicin, pepper fruits have a powerful antibacterial and antiviral effect - they kill up to 75% of harmful bacteria. Increases immunity, especially resistance to various colds. A small amount of chili stimulates the digestive tract and increases appetite. Reduces blood sugar levels. Improves eyesight.

Bush Type Pears

Red pepper is very hot! A number of precautions must be observed when eating it. When cutting and processing chili fruits, it is recommended to wear gloves, since some especially hot varieties can burn the skin at the slightest contact. Avoid contact of pepper with mucous membranes.
